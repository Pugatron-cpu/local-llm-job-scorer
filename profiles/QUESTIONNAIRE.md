# Job-search profile, a few questions

Hi! I run a little tool that scans job boards and scores roles against what someone is
actually looking for, then gives back a shortlist of the good matches. I'd like to run it
for you. To do that well I need a picture of you and what you want. Just answer below in
your own words, no need to be formal or polish anything. Rough and honest beats neat and
vague, the more real detail you give, the better the matches. Takes about ten minutes.

**If you have a CV, attach it.** That covers your background and skills, so you can keep the
"about you" answers short and focus on the parts a CV doesn't say: what you actually *want*,
and your constraints. (Your CV stays with me, it isn't published anywhere.)

(Honesty helps you here: the tool matches against what you tell it, so listing a skill you
don't really have just brings back roles you don't want.)

---

## 1. About you  *(brief if you've attached a CV)*

1. **Your name:**

2. **Where are you right now, work-wise?** (e.g. studying, working and looking to move,
   recently graduated, between jobs, returning to work)

3. **What's your background?** Studies, field, and a sentence or two on what you've done. If
   you've sent a CV, a one-liner is plenty here.

## 2. What you're good at  *(brief if you've attached a CV)*

4. **Your main skills.** Anything relevant, technical or not (e.g. Excel, SQL, Python,
   bookkeeping, customer service, project coordination, languages, a design tool). List what
   you'd genuinely be comfortable being asked about.

5. **Roughly how much experience?** (e.g. "2 years in retail", "student, no work experience
   yet", "5 years in admin")

## 3. The role you want  *(the important part, please do fill this in)*

6. **What kind of role are you after?** (e.g. data analyst, office/admin, IT support,
   customer service, something at a tech company as a way in, open to a few things)

7. **Job titles you'd actually type into a job site.** Give me 4 to 8, in your language and
   in English if you can (e.g. "studentermedhjælper", "data analyst", "kontorassistent",
   "junior project coordinator").

8. **Hours and type.** How many hours a week, and what type? (full-time / part-time /
   student job / internship / flexible)

9. **Anything you do NOT want?** (e.g. no sales, no night shifts, no phone-heavy roles)

## 4. Where, and how far

10. **Which town/city do you live in?**

11. **How far would you travel for work, and how?** (e.g. "up to 45 min by train/metro",
    "only places I can bike to", "happy to drive 30 min") And which nearby towns or areas
    would count as reasonable?

12. **Is fully remote work something you'd take?** (yes / no / only partly)

## 5. Languages

13. **Which languages do you speak, and how well?** This matters because a lot of local job
    ads are in the local language. (e.g. "Danish, fluent", "Danish, basic", "English only")

## 6. Anything else

14. **What's the goal behind this search?** (e.g. first job in a new field, more hours, a
    foot in the door somewhere, a change of scene) A line is enough.

15. **Anything else I should know?** Constraints, must-haves, a start date, whatever.

---

Send it back however's easiest, typed answers, an email, even a voice note. Thanks!
